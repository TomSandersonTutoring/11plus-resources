Non-Verbal Reasoning

Puzzles made of shapes and patterns, with no words at all. Once you know what to look for, they turn from baffling into rather satisfying.

Non-verbal reasoning swaps words for shapes. Every puzzle is a small pattern to crack: something is changing from one picture to the next, and your job is to spot exactly what, then carry it on or use it. There’s no reading and no vocabulary, just careful looking.

None of this is taught in school, so nearly every child meets it cold. That’s the good news, really: because it’s all about familiarity, a little time with each type makes a real difference. The exam won’t tell you in advance which types will appear, so it’s worth meeting all of them at least once. Pick a topic below and start whenever you like.

Worth knowing: there are only a handful of things these puzzles ever change, and nearly every question is one of them: size, shape, shading, how many, position, or rotation. Once you get into the habit of checking each one in turn, most questions come apart quickly. We’ll point them out as we go.

A friendly place to start

Odd ones out & matches

Spot the shape that doesn’t belong, or the one that fits best with a group. It’s the most natural way in, since you’re simply comparing pictures, and it builds the habit of noticing small differences that every other type leans on.
